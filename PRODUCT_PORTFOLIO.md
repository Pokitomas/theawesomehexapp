# Product Portfolio Constitution

This is the commercial and product-allocation companion to `product/product-portfolio.json` and `scripts/product-portfolio.mjs`. It decides what each surviving surface is, who it is for, why it deserves investment, what gets merged or demoted, and what evidence must exist before stronger market claims are allowed.

The dollar ranges are planning hypotheses for this exact repository state. They are not an appraisal, financing promise, acquisition offer, or claim that unfinished research already has product-market fit.

## The decision

There are **two commercial product families**, not five unrelated apps.

1. **Archie + Maker** are one intelligence-and-execution family. Archie owns ambition, context, continuity, coordination, learning proposals, and capability selection. Maker owns bounded mutation, exact-base work, verification, receipts, delivery, and rollback. They may remain different surfaces because they serve different moments, but they should not be funded, marketed, or valued as unrelated companies.
2. **Sideways** is the independent consumer product. The public root and `/manual/` are two entrances to one promise: discover through the world, then keep and shape your own world.

**Founder is not a standalone product.** It remains a cheap internal instrument for exposing competing directions, preserving rationale, and feeding the next experiment.

**Expo is withheld** as a public product until working visual, sonic, speech, music, and persistent-world commissions produce raw physical-device evidence and survive independent scoring. An issue tree is not a fifth app.

Capital allocation:

| Area | Share | Reason |
|---|---:|---|
| Archie + Maker | 55% | Largest upside and deepest technical moat, but also the largest remaining intelligence and reliability gap. |
| Sideways | 30% | Strongest current consumer-shaped product and the fastest route to real retention, ownership, and paid demand. |
| Expo evidence | 12% | Funds multimodal exhibits, physical-device traces, and the promotion tournament without pretending research is already a product. |
| Founder instrument | 3% | Keep it useful, local, and cheap; do not build a company around it. |

## Every live path has one job

| Path | What it is | Use it for | Who it is for |
|---|---|---|---|
| `/` | Sideways public discovery | Find public material through transparent ranking and enter the owned archive. | Curious readers, community members, researchers, and people seeking better discovery. |
| `/manual/` | Sideways private home | Import, save, arrange, revisit, back up, and restore personal material. | Online archivists, creators, community builders, and people leaving closed platforms. |
| `/archie/` | Archie personal atelier | State what should exist, gather context and boundaries, preserve continuity, and produce an objective packet. | Privacy-sensitive power users, creators, founders, and small expert teams. |
| `/maker/` | Maker software workshop | Author an exact-base engineering run with explicit authority, proof, rollback, and delivery boundaries. | Software teams, technical founders, agencies, and regulated engineering organizations. |
| `/founder/` | Founder decision instrument | Compare incompatible product directions, react, preserve rationale, and export a deterministic receipt. | Internal product teams, design partners, and independent evaluators. |

The missing `/expo/` path is deliberate. Expo earns a public route only after issue #471’s lived commissions, issue #472’s real-device evidence, and issue #473’s independent promotion tournament exist.

## Archie

**What it is:** a resident personal intelligence and coordination layer. It is not architecturally a chat app, voice assistant, coding-model brand, or dashboard. Its exact embodiment must come from the strongest admitted model, faculties, permissions, hardware, latency, continuity, privacy, and resource envelope.

**Why push it:** this is the highest-ceiling product. A trustworthy local intelligence that can preserve context, coordinate work, inspect its own evidence, and operate through user-owned state is meaningfully different from a prompt window. The existing repository already has an installable runtime shell, objective packets, native workspaces, model admission law, capability-frontier selection, and an evidence-bound distillation pipeline.

**Why it is not launched as “genius” yet:** no independently admitted generally capable model exists in the repository. Interface craft, deterministic declared-suite substitution, and training plumbing do not establish intelligence.

**Who pays:** privacy-sensitive professionals, independent creators, technical founders, and small teams that need persistent context and attributable action.

**Pricing hypothesis:** $25–$60 monthly personal, $80–$250 monthly professional, or $15,000–$100,000 annual team agreements plus compute.

**Value hypothesis:**

- current code and research asset: **$50,000–$250,000**;
- after one admitted model and 100 retained paying users: **$3 million–$12 million**;
- after repeatable paid team outcomes: **$15 million–$75 million**.

The largest value jump requires independent hidden evaluation, clean-machine reproduction, real-device receipts, retained payers, and measured completion and repair—not another design pass.

## Maker

**What it is:** the permissioned software execution and evidence engine of the Archie family. It turns an exact end state into one leased, reviewable, verified change set with explicit authority, cost, provenance, and rollback.

**Why push it:** the market already pays materially for AI coding agents and enterprise controls. Maker’s strongest differentiation is not “another coding chat”; it is exact-base execution, one-writer authority, independent verification, retained negative evidence, delivery receipts, and rollback.

**Why merge it commercially with Archie:** ordinary users should not have to understand a separate agent brand before their objective becomes work. Archie decides and coordinates; Maker executes. The execution surface remains separately inspectable for professionals and enterprises.

**Who pays:** software teams, agencies, technical founders, and organizations that need auditable automation rather than unbounded code generation.

**Pricing hypothesis:** $40–$200 per active user monthly, usage and compute up to roughly $1,000 monthly for power users, and $25,000–$250,000 annual enterprise agreements.

**Value hypothesis:**

- current code and research asset: **$100,000–$500,000**;
- after at least 80% independent task success: **$5 million–$20 million**;
- after repeatable renewed enterprise pilots: **$20 million–$100 million**.

Open issues #447, #448, and #450 are direct blockers. A product that cannot reliably compose its orchestrator, plugin registry, and runtime platform is not ready for an enterprise reliability claim.

## Sideways

**What it is:** an owned personal archive and sideways discovery network. It is not a second Archie memory substrate, generic infinite feed, or cloud-only bookmark manager.

**Why push it:** this is the strongest present-day consumer product in the repository. It already has private import, profiles, posts, media, places, deterministic discovery, backup, restore, and optional public authority. It can create value before a frontier model is trained.

The root and `/manual/` must stop competing as separate product identities. The root is the public doorway; `/manual/` is the owned home.

**Who pays:** online archivists, creators, community builders, research-heavy readers, and people migrating away from closed platforms.

**Pricing hypothesis:** free local ownership, $8–$25 monthly sync and hosting, and $25–$75 monthly creator or community tiers.

**Value hypothesis:**

- current code and research asset: **$75,000–$350,000**;
- after 10,000 retained monthly users: **$3 million–$15 million**;
- after paid retention and healthy network growth: **$15 million–$80 million**.

The decisive evidence is next-day and month-three return, successful import and restore, willingness to pay for sync, recognizable authorship, and healthy communities that do not converge into one template.

## Founder

**What it is:** a local decision and product-tournament instrument.

**Why retain it:** it can cheaply preserve rejected directions and explicit judgment, which reduces product amnesia and premature convergence.

**Why not push it as a company:** it has no independent distribution, collaboration, retention, or willingness-to-pay evidence. The standalone company value is currently **$0**. Its current code and research utility is approximately **$5,000–$25,000**, with possible strategic uplift only when it demonstrably improves promotion decisions.

Founder should be bundled into Archie professional work and Expo evaluations. No independent growth team, subscription, or brand campaign.

## Expo

**What it is supposed to become:** an independent multimodal capability exposition and promotion tournament where visual, sonic, speech, music, and persistent-world capability is experienced through meaningful commissions before evidence is inspected.

**Why fund it:** if completed truthfully, it can become a research moat, enterprise evaluation product, and the mechanism that discovers unfamiliar computing forms rather than merely copying current interfaces.

**Why it is withheld:** there are not yet working exhibits, physical-device raw traces, independent scorers, portable server-independent exports, or blinded human preference and authorship results.

**Pricing hypothesis after proof:** $50,000–$250,000 evaluation programs or $100,000–$1 million annual research partnerships. Consumer subscription value remains **$0** until a real recurring consumer use emerges.

**Value hypothesis:**

- current issue tree and research asset: **$0–$100,000**;
- after a reproducible multimodal pavilion: **$1 million–$10 million**;
- after independent enterprise adoption: **$10 million–$50 million**.

## What is deleted conceptually now

- Every URL is no longer treated as a separate product or company.
- The root ranking laboratory and `/manual/` are no longer separate Sideways brands.
- Maker is no longer a standalone mass-market consumer app.
- Founder no longer receives independent product investment.
- Expo no longer receives launch language merely because its campaign issues exist.
- No polished interface can raise the capability claim above the evidence.

The files and URLs remain because they still perform useful jobs. Deletion means removing false ontology and investment, not breaking valid paths for theatrical simplification.

## Market anchors

These anchors constrain pricing; they do not prove product value.

- Cursor’s official pricing currently places individual AI coding around $20 monthly, teams around $40 per user monthly, and high-usage tiers much higher: `https://cursor.com/pricing`.
- Devin’s official self-serve documentation lists free, $20 Pro, $200 Max, and team full-seat pricing around $40 monthly with an $80 team minimum: `https://docs.devin.ai/admin/billing/self-serve`.
- Notion’s official pricing places its AI business workspace around $20 per member monthly and sells agent work through additional credits: `https://www.notion.com/pricing`.
- Raindrop.io demonstrates the free-local-plus-paid-archive model for personal collections: `https://raindrop.io/pro/buy`.

## Executable use

```bash
npm run product:portfolio -- validate
npm run product:portfolio -- routes
npm run product:portfolio -- market
npm run product:portfolio -- route /archie/
npm run test:portfolio
```

The validator rejects duplicate paths, missing audiences, unsupported investment decisions, malformed value ranges, capital allocation that does not total 100%, a visible surface without a route, and any public Expo route while Expo remains explicitly withheld.
