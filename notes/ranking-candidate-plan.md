# Ranking candidate pass

Replace half of the root reader’s candidate surface with social-native records while preserving the automatic saturation policy.

Planned sources:

- archive journalism: English Wikinews, filtered to 2009–2019 and youth-relevant cultural, technology, and economic topics;
- social-native candidate layer: reproducible historical public-discussion records and synthetic interaction metadata, with source-specific attribution and explicit prototype labeling;
- current-2010s-revival layer: deterministic bridge records connecting archived artifacts to present-day revival motives without pretending archival text is current.

The feed must distinguish archive publication date, resurfacing date, and synthetic ranking metadata. It must not present one million candidates as one million unique posts or imply that ranking-laboratory records are canonical Sideways social publications.
