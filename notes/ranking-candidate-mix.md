# Ranking candidate mix

Target root-reader candidate pool:

- 50% archive journalism candidates
- 50% social-native candidates

The split applies to candidate generation, not canonical authority or unique-source count. The ranker may depart from 50/50 per slate after saturation-state adaptation.

## Temporal fit

Archive journalism is filtered toward 2009–2019 and weighted toward technology, music, film, games, fashion, youth economics, labor, internet culture, urban life, and consumer systems. Hard-news records outside that window remain eligible only when strongly connected to a current 2010s-revival motive.

Every resurfaced record carries both `publishedAt` and `resurfacedAt`. The UI must never imply an archival story is newly reported.

## Social-shaped candidates

These records carry post text, community, pseudonymous author label, score/comment metadata, created date, reply depth, outbound link where available, and prototype embeddings/classifier fields. They are ranking-laboratory inputs, not authenticated Sideways publications. Any synthetic bridge record is labeled as generated prototype content and never attributed to a real user.

Canonical Sideways publications come only from the public social authority. A candidate response may determine delivery eligibility; it may not create, mutate, or delete public authority.
