The interior of a small mind

Archie is a from-scratch neural router — no big model, no cloud, no text generation. It reads what you say and chooses one of twelve dispositions, then forms a fixed protocol of intent. This app doesn't put it to work. It lets you watch it think.

The dial is its real softmax — twelve impulses competing at once. The glow that survives is its choice. Words in your sentence are tinted by leave-one-out attribution: how much each one actually pulled the decision. When nothing coheres it routes to clarify and refuses — not a failure, a boundary it keeps.

Reverie lets it off the leash: it free-associates from the bigram shape of its own vocabulary and routes its own drifting thoughts. Unprompted. The being, idling.
