# Sideways: public intelligence for people

Sideways is not a coding assistant, an enterprise data ontology, a rocket company, a chatbot skin, or an agent dashboard.

Sideways is being built as a **person- and community-owned operating system for intelligence-directed work**: turn an outcome into an evidence-bound program of understanding, coordination, permissioned action, durable artifacts, and reusable learning.

The ambition is to reach the general capacity underneath products such as Cursor, Palantir, and SpaceX without inheriting their enclosure or mission:

- Cursor compresses intent into software changes.
- Palantir compresses fragmented institutional data into operational decisions.
- SpaceX compresses sensing, engineering, execution, failure, and iteration into one compounding institution.
- Sideways must generalize that capacity across human knowledge and collective action while keeping memory, authority, provenance, and learned procedure legible and portable to the people involved.

This is a north star, not a present capability claim.

## One product, several organs

**Sideways is the consumer product and the human-facing name.** The repository's other names are internal organs or developer surfaces, not rival consumer identities.

- **Sideways** owns the person's interface, private archive, public discovery, communities, missions, evidence, approvals, receipts, and portable memory.
- **Archie** is the derivational intelligence engine: persistent learned state, typed operators, uncertainty, routing, teacher distillation, and proof-carrying plans.
- **Maker** is the only permissioned effect executor: tools, leases, mutation, verification, rollback, and terminal artifacts.
- **Foundry** is capability science: competing hypotheses, matched experiments, hidden evaluation, resource accounting, checkpoint admission, and rejection.
- **Weave** is coordination: authenticated work state, multi-participant synthesis, delegation, replay, and termination.
- **Social** is shared human authority: identity, communities, conversation, governance, moderation, appeals, and collective missions.
- **Ark** is survival: user-owned export, restoration, portability, and death-resistant memory.

A phone, desktop, terminal, browser, or local runtime may expose different controls, but they must present one coherent Sideways system rather than turning every subsystem into a separate app brand.

## The universal outcome loop

Every meaningful Sideways journey should eventually traverse one inspectable loop:

1. **Ask** — A person or community states an outcome, not a sequence of app clicks.
2. **Map** — Sideways assembles the relevant private context, public sources, connected-tool state, community knowledge, constraints, and uncertainty.
3. **Derive** — Archie proposes explanations, hypotheses, plans, alternate paths, and the boundaries of what it does not know.
4. **Challenge** — Foundry, other agents, or affected people attack the plan, compare alternatives, and expose missing evidence or illegitimate assumptions.
5. **Authorize** — Sideways shows consequential actions, affected parties, costs, reversibility, and required grants before anything happens.
6. **Act** — Maker executes only the authorized plan across connected tools and institutions.
7. **Prove** — The system returns artifacts, receipts, changed state, failures, rollback information, and unresolved uncertainty.
8. **Remember** — Verified procedures, negative lessons, and causal evidence become portable user- or community-owned capability.
9. **Organize** — People may share, fork, govern, or coordinate around the resulting knowledge and procedures without silently surrendering private history.
10. **Repeat** — New outcomes begin from a better state instead of reinstalling intelligence from zero.

The repository should delete or demote features that do not strengthen this loop.

## What intelligence-directed work means

The primary mission is not rockets, weapons, surveillance, advertising optimization, or replacing one enterprise dashboard with another.

Sideways should help people and communities:

- understand a confusing world;
- investigate claims and institutions;
- preserve personal and public memory;
- learn and teach;
- create software, media, research, and culture;
- navigate bureaucracy and connected tools;
- coordinate mutual aid and community projects;
- design, debate, and evaluate policy;
- repair broken systems;
- discover transferable methods;
- turn successful work into shared public capacity.

The system may support industrial or scientific work, but its sovereign objective is **human and civic agency**, not a privileged organization's private command capacity.

## Capacity target

Sideways should eventually be:

- **broader than Cursor** — capable of deriving and executing any authorized digital workflow, not only editing code;
- **more legitimate than Palantir** — able to connect private, public, and community state without centralizing ownership, hiding provenance, or removing appeal;
- **more universal than SpaceX** — able to create fast, compounding, evidence-driven institutions for thousands of missions rather than vertically integrating one physical program;
- **more cumulative than a chatbot** — every verified result can become owned procedure, while every failure can become negative knowledge;
- **more inspectable than an autonomous agent** — consequential action remains permissioned, receipt-bound, reversible where possible, and attributable.

Beating those systems does not mean copying their interface or market. It means surpassing their ability to convert information into coordinated, compounding capacity while changing who owns that capacity and what it is for.

## Product invariants

1. **The person or community is sovereign.** Private memory, learned skills, grants, and exports remain theirs.
2. **The feed is not sovereign.** Ranking is a delivery function, never the owner of identity, memory, conversation, or truth.
3. **Private history is not free training exhaust.** It enters learning only through explicit, provenance-preserving admission.
4. **Public conversation is governed social authority, not a scrape pile.** Communities retain membership, moderation, appeal, and fork boundaries.
5. **Evidence precedes claims.** Sources, uncertainty, contradiction, and causal receipts remain visible.
6. **Consequential action requires authority.** Archie proposes; Maker executes; people grant or deny.
7. **Capability claims require empirical admission.** Unit tests and fixture success do not prove model quality.
8. **Learning must compound without silent self-modification.** Mutable learned state is separated from immutable authority and runtime boundaries.
9. **Portability defeats enclosure.** Archives, procedures, manifests, receipts, and model artifacts must have inspectable export and recovery paths.
10. **No component-brand fragmentation.** Archie, Maker, Foundry, Weave, Social, and Ark serve one Sideways outcome loop.
11. **No primary optimization for coercion.** Mass surveillance, weapons targeting, behavioral manipulation, and engagement extraction are rejected as organizing product missions.
12. **No mythology in place of reality.** Every surface states what is local, simulated, server-backed, independently measured, or still missing.

## The first impossible proof

The decisive demonstration is not a chatbot conversation and not an autonomous code change.

A person or community should be able to state a difficult public outcome—for example:

> Make this dangerous intersection safer.

Sideways should then be able to:

- assemble local testimony, crash records, maps, public meetings, agency responsibility, budgets, and prior plans;
- distinguish fact, allegation, uncertainty, and missing records;
- identify affected people and legitimate decision-makers;
- propose several intervention paths with costs and tradeoffs;
- draft records requests, testimony, outreach, petitions, design alternatives, and implementation plans;
- ask before contacting anyone, spending money, publishing, or modifying connected systems;
- coordinate residents and experts through governed communities;
- track agency replies and changed world state;
- retain the successful procedure as a portable civic skill;
- publish reusable public evidence without exposing private participant history.

The same machinery should transfer to education, employment, health navigation, science, software, journalism, local government, creative production, and institutional repair.

## Current repository truth

The repository already contains substantial pieces of this system:

- a user-owned private archive with explicit backup and restoration;
- a canonical public social authority with communities, governance, moderation, appeals, and receipts;
- an inspectable ranking and eligibility substrate;
- Maker's bounded engineering execution and verification path;
- Archie's symbolic-relational planning, memory, artifact runtime, and evaluation substrate;
- Foundry calibration and deterministic campaign allocation;
- Weave coordination and authenticated work-state machinery.

It does **not** yet contain the complete universal outcome product, a broadly capable admitted Archie checkpoint, ordinary connected-tool execution across a person's life, or independently demonstrated superiority to Cursor, Palantir, SpaceX, or frontier models.

## Execution order

The highest-leverage path is:

1. keep Sideways as the single consumer identity and unify its currently fragmented journeys;
2. introduce a durable outcome/mission object that binds goal, evidence, actors, permissions, plans, actions, receipts, and learned procedure;
3. connect ordinary tools and institutions through explicit adapters and Maker grants;
4. make private memory and community knowledge useful without collapsing their authority boundaries;
5. train and independently evaluate a provider-neutral Archie student that materially improves outcome completion;
6. admit only checkpoints that beat simple baselines without regressing safety, authority, cost, latency, portability, or artifact integrity;
7. expose community missions, reusable civic skills, and public-interest intelligence commons;
8. package the local runtime and Sideways experience so a normal person can install, understand, use, inspect, export, and recover it.

The destination is not a smarter interface to existing institutions. It is infrastructure through which ordinary people can build, govern, and retain institutions of intelligence themselves.
